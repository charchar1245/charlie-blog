My First Steps Into Machine Learning

Two weeks ago, my Algorithms professor recommended that I explore machine learning and AI. Before that moment I had almost no experience — I just knew it was important and rapidly growing. That initial curiosity quickly turned into genuine interest as I began to understand how these models actually learn.

Building Foundational Knowledge

I started with Andrew Ng’s "Machine Learning Specialization," where I learned the basics: regression, gradient descent, classification, and the foundations behind training a model. His explanations made difficult concepts surprisingly intuitive.

Visual Intuition & Neural Networks

The visual explanations from Coding Train and 3Blue1Brown helped me understand neural networks on an intuitive level. Seeing activations, weights, and decision boundaries change during training made everything feel *alive*. This was the point where machine learning stopped being abstract and started becoming something I genuinely enjoyed exploring.

I have detailed reports of the projects on my GitHub page for both my Housing Prices Predictor and my Diabetes Classification Model. The key take-away is this: by starting with small, focused projects you can set building blocks for larger, cooler ones.

Stay tuned.
Charlie Gottschalk

A New Section

As I progressed further, I began exploring PyTorch seriously — learning tensors, shaping data, debugging layers, testing optimizers, and designing my own models from scratch. This was where machine learning stopped being "learning" and started becoming "building."

Every mistake taught me something. Every small success — even a tiny bump in accuracy — kept me going. Machine learning is a field where curiosity compounds… and it becomes very addictive.
